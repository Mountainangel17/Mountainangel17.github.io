My life title should be Loving Chaos,at least that is what it looks like from the outside...But I really believe that other people's version of chaos is my version of simply trying to create the best possible existence for my kids and myself.

You may ask yourself...well, how did I get here?!

I started out my adult version with the expectation of being a field photographer for Life magazine.

At the end of my junior year at college I was informed that my financial aid was to be eliminated, thanks to the shakedown occuring in Washington via the Whitehouse. A shakedown fondly termed Reaganomics.

In a heated moment I decided to join the military, my logic being that the GI Bill would help me finish my degree in photojournalism.

I signed up to be an Air Traffic Controller because the technology of the work appealed to me. The day that I finished Basic Training I was handed orders to go to school to be a combat medic/radiologic technologist. I was horrified, there was nothing I could imagine doing less! I did not consider myself a healthcare-type individual. This was the day that I learned that my mother was an illegal alien, and because of that I was not eligible for top secret clearance...and that job.

So that is what I did for 32 years. Kids and mortgages and divorce and life happened and it was simply not economically feasible to be anything but a medical imaging specialist, and x-ray tech, while I was the single mom of 4 kids.

In 2013 I fell. I had been working with a very damaged back since 1986 when I was hurt in the military...but when I fell it knocked my back completely out of alignment permanenetly and I lost all feeling in my right leg. I was forced to retire from my medical career because I walked with a cane and there are physical requirements to the job. I was forced to recreate myself and in doing so I have reverted back to the old creative person I was 3 decades ago. I lost my house and am living very humbly. I have learned that when you have nothing left to lose...you discover how much you really have.

Why Web Development?

This is a very different world than the one where my dreams lie. Magazines and newspapers are essentially gone. The internet and the communications that have evolved with it are the reality now. I am still intrigued by the technology, and frankly super excited by where photography has evolved to. But I have so much to learn! The journey ahead of me is long and will be very bumpy, but expanding on what I love and not what I need to do will make the journey on of personal fullfillment and immense satisfaction.

1991: Tim Berners-Lee publishes “HTML Tags” which describes the initial 18 elements of HTML

1992: NCSA develops the Mosaic browser, which will eventually evolve into Netscape

1994: The Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) creates an HTML working group to develop HTML specifications. Later that year the W3C was created to foster an open standards environment

1995: HTML 2.0 specification is published

1995: Microsoft releases Internet Explorer to compete with Netscape

1997: HTML 3.2 specification is published

1997: HTML 4.0 specification is published

2000: XHTML 1.0 is published as a W3C recommendation

2004: The WHATWG forms to continue work on HTML

2006: W3C announces it will work with the WHATWG on HTML5

2009: XHTML Working Group charter expires

2012: W3C and WHATWG announce they will develop the HTML5 standard separately
